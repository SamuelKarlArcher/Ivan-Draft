Dr L.M Archers Professional Summary

My medical training started at the age of 18 years in Zimbabwe (then Rhodesia). I spent a year in active combat as an army medic, which led me into the field of medicine. Zimbabwe medical care collapsed, resulting myself, wife and two children immigrating to South Africa. My present practice has served an isolated rural community of approximately 10 000 people from the poorest to richest for past 37 years.

Work History

01/1983 to 12/1983 Houseman, Medical, Pediatric, Orthopedics, Surgery Harare Hospital and Parirenyatwa Hospitals – Harare, ZIMABABWE

The largest teaching hospital in Zimbabwe, advanced trauma specialization with approximately 2000 beds. Critical shortage of doctors at the time meant that junior doctors practiced above normal expectations.

02/1984 to 01/1987 Resident Medical Officer Ladysmith Provincial Hospital – Ladysmith, KZN.

adysmith hospital is an 800-bed hospital that serves as the regional referral center for Northern KZN. Surgical Resident 5 months. General surgery, facial reconstruction, thoracotomies, trauma, Intensive Care. Rapid response with local emergency services to major incidents. Anesthetics Residents 7 months. General, spinal, epidural and regional blocks. Obstetrics and Gynecology 6 months. Caesarian Sections, vacuum and forceps deliveries, complicated deliveries. Pediatric resuscitation, neonatal care. Head of casualty responsible for the startup of creating a dedicated casualty and theatre. Training and mentorship of junior doctors in anesthetics and minor surgical procedures. MUA, I&D, EVAC, Lumps, biopsies, minor plastic surgical procedures, Abscess etc. Major trauma resuscitation. This resulted in reducing the need for admission into main hospital by up to 20%. 02/1987 to 07/1987 Radiology Registrar Groote Schuur Hospital – Cape Town, Professor Kottler for 4 years specializing in Radiology. Radiology, X rays, CT scans, MRI, ultrasound and fundamentals of radiology. I did 6 months then resigned as I realized I missed general medicine and patient contact. Decided to return to work in a Zimbabwe mission hospital Bonda, as doctor in charge. After 2 months of bureaucratic red tape, it was clear this dream would not be realized. Developing a private hospital with massive community support was also blocked by President Robert Mugabe.

09/1987 to 11/1987 Resident Medical Officer Ladysmith Provincial Hospital – Ladysmith, KZN

In charge of Casualty department

11/1987 to Current General Practitioner, District Surgeon Self and provincial administration – Colenso, Kwa Zulu Natal

Practice serves 6-10 thousand people in the area. District Surgeon duties. Ensuring the medical needs of pensioners and social welfare government cases were met. Medico legal work included postmortems, rape, drunken driving, assault cases, prisoners, appearance for the state in court. Family planning services and Disability assessments. This was for 10 years, about 2 to 3 hours a day. Occupational Health 1988 to 1994. Occupational Health doctor at Frame Industrial Textiles (Cotton), Transnet (Railways), Eskom(electricity), +/- 5000 employees. Preplacement, annual medicals and exit medical examination. IOD and medical boarding. Occupational Disease management. Specialized in occupational health at University KZN. Two-year Diploma in Occupational Health DOH. Meritorious award for Research project. Shareholder and founder member of construction and starting La Verna Private Hospital in Ladysmith. Responsible for purchasing of equipment and staffing of radiology department, running and doing radiologist role, IVP, venograms and reporting on x-rays, done at no charge as not a qualified radiologist. This was done for the first year, until a Radiologist started. For 15 years at La Verna private hospital I did anesthetics part time, hospital management of patients in obstetrics and Gyne, pediatrics, general medicine, surgery such as appendicectomy, trauma and emergency medicine. Course done to provide mental health care. At my general practice in Colenso, I built a casualty room for treatment of major trauma, including stab and gunshot wounds. Went to many local farm attacks to attend to multiple GSWs and serious multiple injury MVA. The practice also does Ultrasound, minor surgical procedure and handles A to Z problems as I am the only medical center in Colenso. Home deliveries, anesthetics, surgery and specialist type work has been stopped as cannot afford medical protection insurance. Still do antenatal care and emergencies. 2023 I renewed mt advanced cardiovascular support course. Advanced pediatric support is done but needs renewal. Occupational health care for factories such as Tugela steel, a steel fabrication workshop which builds large steel structures. Fully equipped pharmacy is available and in 2004 this required me to do South African dispensing course and license, allowing me to dispense medicine. The practice offers a family planning service. I have completed a Travel Medicine course and am a Travel clinic. The practice has always done vaccinations, including yellow fever. Completed Impairment assessment course involving the American Impairment assessment methodology. As district surgeon I did large number of Disability Assessments for Government. The practice also helps with orphanage and has a strong community upliftment education program to deal with issues from farm attacks to the assessment of the safety measures necessary for the zip line canopy tours run in the Drakensberg.
